Exhibits

Nefertiti

The famous queen known for her exquisite beauty. She played a key role in religious and artistic reforms alongside her husband, Akhenaten.

Tutankhamun

The youngest pharaoh of Egypt, he ruled as a child. His golden tomb, discovered in 1922, remains a symbol of Ancient Egypt’s grandeur.

Pyramids of Giza

Timeless Egyptian wonders built to honor the pharaohs, representing genius engineering and enduring legacy.

Ancient Egyptian Papyrus

A scroll made from the papyrus plant, used for writing and recording religious texts, official documents, and daily life. Each papyrus is a window into the rich culture and wisdom of Ancient Egypt.

Rosetta Stone

A stone slab that helped decode hieroglyphics, featuring inscriptions in three scripts: Hieroglyphic, Demotic, and Greek.

Khufu Solar Boat

A wooden boat used in royal funerary rituals, believed to transport the king to the afterlife.

Mummy of Ramses II

The most famous royal mummy, remarkably preserved, belonging to Ramses II, one of Egypt’s greatest pharaohs.

Narmer Palette

A stone palette depicting the unification of Upper and Lower Egypt, one of the world’s oldest historical records.

Statue of Akhenaten

A statue of Pharaoh Akhenaten, founder of monotheism, portrayed in a unique artistic style reflecting religious change.

Ancient Egyptian Medical Papyrus

A papyrus containing detailed medical treatments for fractures and wounds, showing the advancement of ancient Egyptian medicine.

Statue of the Seated Scribe

A statue representing respect for knowledge, showing a seated scribe with realistic inlaid eyes.

Tutankhamun’s Chariot

An impressive royal chariot used in ceremonies and possibly battles, showcasing ancient Egyptian craftsmanship.

Statue of Hatshepsut

A statue of Queen Hatshepsut, one of Egypt’s greatest rulers, depicted with royal authority and dignity.

Statue of Amenhotep III and Queen Tiye

A royal family statue highlighting the importance of women and family in ancient Egypt.

Eye of Horus

An ancient symbol of protection, healing, and power, often worn by kings and placed inside mummies.

Seated Statue of Senusret III

A statue with realistic facial features reflecting the king’s responsibility and devotion to ruling.

Kadesh Battle Relief

A relief describing the Battle of Kadesh between Ramses II and the Hittites, considered the oldest documented battle.

Stone Coffin of Tutankhamun

A quartzite coffin decorated with religious inscriptions to protect the king in the afterlife.

Sacred Scarab Amulet

A sacred amulet symbolizing rebirth, renewal, and protection, widely used in burials and rituals.

Abu Simbel Temple

Rock-cut temple built by Ramses II, famous for the solar alignment phenomenon occurring twice a year.
